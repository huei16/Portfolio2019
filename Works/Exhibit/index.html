塑膠帶 Reuse Plastic Bags

Exhibit Design / 2018

About Project

Inspired by the simPLASTIC project, me and three of my friends decided to curate an exhibition about “Reusing Plastic Bags”. Throughout the process, we really focused on the viewers’ experience; from visuals and interactive display to the actual installation of the exhibition, everything was done by us!

Content

01.

Use Plastic Bags Widely!

Our slogan “Use Plastic Bags Widely” seems like the opposite of what we have always been told. This triggers a question mark and brings up people’s curiosity, attracting them to come see our exhibition.

02.

Paper versus Plastic

Most people think paper bags are more environmental friendly, but is that true? From material processing to product distribution, through visualizing the manufature process of paper bags and plastic bags, we explain why we promote plastic bags.

03.

Notorious Plastic Bags

But... the use of plastic bags are currently restricted for a reason. The 7 billion population consumes 5474 billion plastic bags each year, and these non-degradable plastic bags will forever lie on our planet.

04.

From ‘Use’ to ‘Reuse’

Remeber our slogan “You should use plastic bags widely”? What we’re trying to say is: It’s not the plastic bags themselves nor the manufacturing process that’s bad, it’s the way we utilize them (one-time use) that makes them bad. So why not reuse and make the most out of every plastic bag?

05.

Break the Stereotype

People’s major concern for plastic bags are their durability and look. At the exhibition we showcased how plastic bags can hold 3 times the weight than what the same size paper bag holds. There’s also a fashion gallery with images of people pulling off their plastic bag accessories!

06.

Take Actions!

The door curtain at the exit says “On your marks, get set, Go!” Walking through the curtain is like a manifesto of a new way to use plastic bags! We really hope that poeple will exercise what they saw at our exhibition and be part of the change.

Venue Design

Goal

We understand how the arrangement of space strongly affects the viewing experience. Essentially, we want to make the most out of the relatively small venue that we have since there is a lot to deliver. Key principles that we bore in mind are: ‘No spoiler’ and ‘spread out the text-heavy stages’.

The Thinking

Creating wall in the center parts the venue into two halves, and “2” is the “turning point” where we reveal our main message “Reuse instead of Use”.

We then used our content posters to further divide the space into different sections.

We also made sure that the text-heavy parts and the interactive (fun!) parts are evenly distributed.

No Spoiler

We designed the venue to be a route than an open space, so viewers will naturally see the contents in the designed sequence. To prevent Spoilers, the arrangement of every single display has been carefully           thought out.

Over 100 people visited within 2 days!!
